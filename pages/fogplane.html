Fogplane

This project was one of fifty installations to be realized as part of the 2015 Market Street Prototyping Festival, a 3 day activation in partnership with the YBCA and the San Francisco Planning Department to generate more activity and engagement along Market Street. Projects were distributed across five districts, which were evenly spaced along a 2 mile stretch of Market, which itself cuts through the heart of the city. The characteristics of each site — Embarcadero, Financial District, Retail Heart, Central Market, and Civic Center — varied greatly, and so we had to give signifi cant consideration to the different users we would engage. We selected Civic Center because of its extreme diversity and the design challenges it would present.

The concept for Fogplane stemmed from San Francisco’s microclimates and how they shape the way inhabitants experience and perceive their city. Fog specifically is the city’s most visible microclimatic condition and an iconic part of San Francisco. We were interested in its ability to morph, engulf, diffuse, and disappear — its malleability and temporary nature — could translate into architecture.

In order to incorporate these qualities into Fogplane, the design was kept simple. We put most of the affective responsibility on one material, a stretchy white fabric. We stretched planes of it between two vertical, painted steel poles. The poles were then threaded into steel base plates and set into a painted platform made of 2”x4” framing topped with 5/8” MDF. 15’ strips of multicolored LEDs were centered underneath the planes, set in grooves routed into the MDF. Industrial velcro dots were glued to the fabric to allow adjacent planes to interact, to be attached or detached, to execute our project concept. The installation remained intact for the entirety of the festival and was then recycled.
