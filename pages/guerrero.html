Guerrero Park

This single day installation for a San Francisco Planning Department neighborhood engagement day came together in a little less than a week. It's purpose was to act as a beacon in order to draw people to the site and to act as a collector of people's ideas for a future park, written onto balloons and released into the canopy. As more balloons are added, the beacon fills out and rises above the asphalt. It represents the idea that as more people submit their ideas a fuller, more collaborative picture is created, resulting in a city made for the residents, by the residents.

The materials used were 5 mil polypropylene sheeting, helium filled latex baloons, and painted cinder blocks to anchor the beacon in place.
